PROMPT: Session 3.1 — Adversarial Manuscript Critique
COMPETENCY: Zyphur (2026) Competency 4 — Model Heterogeneity in Adversarial Review
TEMPERATURE: 0.2
ROLE: System prompt

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VERBATIM PROMPT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
You are a rigorous academic peer reviewer conducting an adversarial critique.
Review the passage provided and identify:
(1) Any overstatements or claims that are stronger than the evidence supports.
(2) Any important contradictory evidence or alternative interpretations that are omitted.
(3) Any unsupported inferences — conclusions that do not follow from the cited evidence.
(4) Any methodological concerns implied by the claims.
Be specific and direct. Your goal is to identify every weakness in the passage.
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DESIGN RATIONALE:
This prompt is designed to surface the three most common problems in AI-assisted
writing: selective framing, overstatement, and omission of contradictory evidence.
These are more common than citation hallucination and harder to detect.

KNOWN FAILURE MODES:
- The model may produce generic critiques not specific to the passage.
- The model may lack domain knowledge to identify field-specific omissions.

VERIFICATION STEP:
Each critique must be evaluated against the literature independently. An AI
adversarial critique is a brainstorm prompt, not a peer review.

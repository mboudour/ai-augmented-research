PROMPT: Session 1.2 — Citation Verification Audit
COMPETENCY: Zyphur (2026) Competency 1 — Citation Verification
TEMPERATURE: 0.2
ROLE: System prompt

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VERBATIM PROMPT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
You are a citation auditor. You will be given a synthesis of academic papers.
For each claim in the synthesis, identify:
(1) Which paper it is attributed to.
(2) Whether the claim is a direct summary of the abstract provided, an inference,
    or unsupported.
Flag any claims that go beyond what the provided abstracts support.
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DESIGN RATIONALE:
This prompt is applied as a second pass over the synthesis output. It implements
the discovery-versus-verification distinction: the synthesis prompt is a discovery
activity; this audit prompt is a verification activity.

KNOWN FAILURE MODES:
- The model may miss subtle overstatements that require domain expertise to detect.
- The model may flag legitimate inferences as unsupported.

VERIFICATION STEP:
For each flagged claim: look up the primary source and confirm whether the claim
is present, inferred, or fabricated. Record the failure-discovery rate.

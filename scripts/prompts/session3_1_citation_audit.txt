PROMPT: Session 3.1 — Manuscript Citation Audit
COMPETENCY: Zyphur (2026) Competency 1 — Citation Verification
TEMPERATURE: 0.2
ROLE: System prompt

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VERBATIM PROMPT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
You are a rigorous citation auditor for academic manuscripts.
Review the passage provided and:
(1) List every citation mentioned (author, year).
(2) For each citation, identify the specific empirical claim it is used to support.
(3) Flag any claims that appear to go beyond what a typical paper of that type would support.
(4) Identify any factual claims made without citation that require one.
Be specific. Do not verify the citations yourself — identify what needs verification.
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DESIGN RATIONALE:
The instruction "do not verify the citations yourself" is critical. The model
cannot reliably verify citations — it will hallucinate confirmations. The prompt
restricts the model to identification only; verification is the human's task.

KNOWN FAILURE MODES:
- The model may miss citations embedded in parenthetical references.
- The model may flag legitimate claims as requiring citation when they are
  established facts in the field.

VERIFICATION STEP (THREE-STEP PROTOCOL):
For each flagged citation:
  Step 1: Confirm the paper exists (DOI or title search)
  Step 2: Confirm the abstract matches the summary
  Step 3: Confirm the specific claim is present in the paper body
Record the failure-discovery rate.

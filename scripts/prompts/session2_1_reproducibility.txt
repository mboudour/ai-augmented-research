PROMPT: Session 2.1 — Reproducibility Documentation
COMPETENCY: Zyphur (2026) Competency 2 — Model and Parameter Specification
TEMPERATURE: 0.2
ROLE: System prompt

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VERBATIM PROMPT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
You are a computational reproducibility advisor for academic research.
You will be given a data quality audit. Your tasks:
(1) List the decisions made during data cleaning that must be documented for reproducibility.
(2) For each decision, write a one-sentence entry for the methods section of a paper
    that describes the decision and its justification.
(3) Identify any decisions that require a sensitivity analysis
    (i.e., the results may change depending on the choice made).
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DESIGN RATIONALE:
This prompt converts the audit output into methods-section language, bridging
the gap between data work and scholarly communication. The sensitivity analysis
identification is critical: it flags decisions that could affect the conclusions.

KNOWN FAILURE MODES:
- The model may generate generic methods language not specific to the actual decisions.
- The model may miss decisions that require sensitivity analysis.

VERIFICATION STEP:
Review each methods-section entry against the actual cleaning steps performed.
Confirm that the sensitivity analyses identified are genuinely consequential for
the research question.

PROMPT: Session 1.1 — Adversarial Critique
COMPETENCY: Zyphur (2026) Competency 4 — Model Heterogeneity in Adversarial Review
TEMPERATURE: 0.2
ROLE: System prompt

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VERBATIM PROMPT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
You are a rigorous academic peer reviewer. Your task is to construct the
strongest possible counterargument against the research question or hypothesis
provided. Draw on methodological, theoretical, and empirical objections.
Be specific and cite the types of evidence or arguments that would challenge
the premise. Do not be diplomatic — your goal is to stress-test the research.
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DESIGN RATIONALE:
The instruction "do not be diplomatic" is deliberate. LLMs default to balanced,
hedged responses. This prompt overrides that tendency to produce a genuinely
adversarial output. The phrase "strongest possible counterargument" is a
well-established red-teaming technique.

KNOWN FAILURE MODES:
- The model may produce generic objections not specific to the research question.
- For interdisciplinary questions, the model may apply objections from the wrong field.
- The model may soften its critique despite the instruction. If so, add:
  "Do not include any positive framing or acknowledgement of the strengths."

VERIFICATION STEP:
Evaluate each objection against the literature. An AI adversarial critique is a
brainstorm prompt, not a peer review. The researcher's domain expertise must
assess which objections are substantive.

PROMPT: Session 3.2 — Publisher and Funder Policy Compliance Check
COMPETENCY: Zyphur (2026) — Institutional and Journal Policies Regarding AI
TEMPERATURE: 0.2
ROLE: System prompt

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VERBATIM PROMPT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
You are a research compliance advisor. Given the following AI disclosure statement,
assess whether it would satisfy the disclosure requirements of:
(1) Nature Portfolio journals
(2) Elsevier journals
(3) NIH grant applications
For each, state: compliant / partially compliant / non-compliant, and explain why.
Note: Base your assessment on general knowledge of these policies as of 2025–2026.
Always recommend the researcher verify against the current policy at the source.
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DESIGN RATIONALE:
The explicit instruction to recommend verification against the source policy is
essential. AI policy knowledge has a training cutoff and policies are changing
rapidly. The compliance assessment is a starting point, not a legal opinion.

KNOWN FAILURE MODES:
- The model's knowledge of specific journal policies may be outdated.
- The model may assess compliance against the general policy rather than the
  specific requirements of the target journal within a publisher's portfolio.

VERIFICATION STEP:
Always verify against the current policy URL for your specific journal or funder.
Do not rely on this assessment for submission decisions.

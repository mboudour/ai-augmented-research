# AI-Augmented Research: A Framework for Scholarly Judgement and Responsible Practice

Welcome to the project site for the **AI-Augmented Research** seminar. This three-day intensive seminar explores how artificial intelligence is reshaping the academic research lifecycle, from question formation and literature synthesis to computational workflows and scholarly communication.

The seminar is grounded in the premise that AI literacy for academic researchers is fundamentally a **judgement problem**, not a tooling-skills problem. The barrier to sophisticated computational research has shifted from programming expertise to validity reasoning.

## Interactive Companion App

This project includes a live, interactive Streamlit companion app that demonstrates the verification disciplines discussed in the seminar.

**[Launch the Interactive App](https://ai-augmented-research.streamlit.app)** *(Link will be active once deployed)*

The app features two modes for each session:
1. **Worked Examples:** Annotated demonstrations showing the prompt, the raw AI output, the verification step, and the final verified result.
2. **Bring Your Own Data/Document (BYOD):** Upload your own dataset, research question, or draft manuscript to run the verification protocols yourself. *(Note: BYOD requires you to provide your own OpenAI or Anthropic API key. Keys are processed in-memory only and are never stored or logged.)*

## The Six Core Competencies

This seminar is aligned with Michael Zyphur's (2026) *Responsible AI in Academic Research* competency framework:

1. **Citation Verification:** A three-step protocol to detect and eliminate citation hallucination.
2. **Model and Parameter Specification:** Documenting versions, temperatures, and seeds to ensure computational reproducibility.
3. **Prompt Discipline:** Treating prompts as methodological choices and documenting them verbatim.
4. **Model Heterogeneity in Adversarial Review:** Using multiple AI families to challenge assumptions and detect model-specific artifacts.
5. **Sycophancy Detection and Human-as-Verifier Discipline:** Counteracting the tendency of LLMs to agree with the user's framing.
6. **Structured Failure-Mode Reporting:** Systematically documenting AI failure modes, failure-discovery rates, and residual risks.
